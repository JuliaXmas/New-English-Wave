Facts about education in the UK and USA

A. Formal education typically begins with Kindergarten at age five or six, with an emphasis on socialization in the early years. USA

B. Students take GCSEs at age 16 and can then specialize in 3-4 subjects for A levels. UK

C. Students can apply to numerous universities directly or through the Common Application, often requiring multiple essays. USA

D. International students can expect to pay up to $45,000 a year at public universities and up to $55,000 a year or more at private institutions. USA

E. Children generally begin formalized schooling at age four with a greater early focus on academics. UK
