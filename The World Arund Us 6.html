Business Meeting in the UK:

Setting: A modern office with a conference table, ergonomic chairs, and possibly a view of the city skyline.
Participants: British Business Professionals: Formal attire with an emphasis on professionalism and punctuality.
Directness: Open and direct communication style, with a focus on clarity and efficiency.
Meeting Dynamics:
Agenda: Meetings are agenda-driven, with specific goals and objectives outlined.
Decision Making: Often hierarchical, decisions made by senior management, but input from all levels is encouraged.
Cultural Nuances:
Humor: Light humor may be used to ease tension or build rapport.
Networking: Coffee or tea may be offered during breaks, enhancing informal networking opportunities.

Business Meeting in the USA:

Setting: A contemporary conference room with a large table, comfortable chairs, whiteboards, and modern technology such as video conferencing equipment.
Participants:
American Business Professionals: Business casual or formal attire depending on the company culture, with a focus on professionalism and punctuality.
Diverse Roles: Participants may include team members from various departments, with a focus on collaboration. Meeting Dynamics:
Agenda: Meetings are often structured with a clear agenda and specific time allocations for each topic.
Decision Making: Decision-making can be democratic, with active participation and input from all attendees, though final decisions may rest with higher management.
Cultural Nuances:
Direct Communication: Clear, concise, and direct communication is valued. Questions and feedback are encouraged.
Networking: Meetings may start or end with informal conversations or small talk to build rapport and strengthen team relationships.

Business Meeting in Russia:

Setting: A formal meeting room with heavy curtains, ornate furniture, and possibly a portrait of a historical figure.
Participants:
Russian Business Executives: Formal attire with an emphasis on professionalism and respect for authority.
Hierarchy: Hierarchical structure with clear respect for seniority.
Meeting Dynamics:
Discussion: Meetings can be lively and passionate, with debates and robust exchanges of ideas.
Decision Making: Decisions may involve consensus but often influenced by the senior leader's perspective.
Cultural Nuances:
Relationship Building: Initial meetings may involve more personal conversations to establish trust.
Protocol: Handshakes are common, and it's customary to exchange gifts or small tokens of appreciation.
